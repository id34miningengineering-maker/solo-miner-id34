How It Works

Solo Mining Explained.

01
Plug Into USB
Any USB port. Wall adapter, laptop, or power bank. Mining begins without noticing an increase in energy cost. Low voltage by design.
02
Connect WiFi + BTC Address
A configuration page loads on any mobile device. WiFi credentials and a Bitcoin wallet address entered once. That is the complete setup. No account. No technical background required.
03
The Lottery
Every 1.3 seconds the device submits a hash. Solo mining means operating directly against the network — no pool, no split. A solved block delivers the full reward to the wallet address on file. More miners means more hashrate, more tickets.
04
No Overhead
Traditional Bitcoin mining operations carry electricity bills, hardware depreciation, constant maintenance, strategic selling, and asset management. This device has none of that. Pay once. It runs.
05
Open Source
Open source firmware. The hardware is owned outright. Public pool available at Public-Pool.io — track workers in real time. DIY-friendly by design.
06
The Math
The Bitcoin protocol does not weight by farm size. The math that decides who finds the next block does not care how much was spent. Once connected, the math is solid. The network is open. The only requirement is to plug in.
